Healthcare providers are increasingly using artificial intelligence to summarize clinical notes, flag potential risks, and speed up administrative work.
At St. Anne's Hospital, physicians and nurses have started testing a model-assisted triage workflow that helps prioritize urgent cases while reducing documentation time.
The project team said the system still requires human review, especially when symptoms are ambiguous or when patient history is incomplete.
Analysts in Boston and New York noted that the biggest challenge is not model accuracy alone, but workflow design, governance, and patient privacy.
Hospital leadership expects the pilot to improve turnaround time, but staff members want clear rules for accountability before broader rollout.
